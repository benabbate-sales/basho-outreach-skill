# Partner / Ecosystem Outreach — Basho-Style Template

**Use:** First-touch to an analyst, consultant, vendor, or industry figure adjacent to the user's market. Goal is to open a two-way relationship — information exchange, possible referrals, possible joint work.
**Style:** Basho framework adapted for peer-to-peer professional outreach. Not a sales email — but still needs to earn the meeting.

---

## TEMPLATE

**Subject:** [Their specific area of work — e.g. "Your recent note on [named topic]"]

Hi [First name],

[Specific reference: a report they authored, a talk they gave, a view they've publicly taken on the space, or a client outcome they've shared. One or two sentences. Show you've actually read it.]

I'm [your role] at [your firm] — [one-line position of what you do in this market]. We see the same problem you do, from [your angle on it]: [one specific observation from your firm's work that connects to their view]. We work with [Client A] and [Client B], among others.

Rather than pitch anything, I'd like 20 minutes to trade notes — where you're seeing the market move, what patterns are showing up on our side. Is [specific day/time, e.g. "Wednesday at 4pm"] workable, or would the week after suit better?

Best,
[Your first name]

---

## SECTION-BY-SECTION NOTES

**Subject.** A named piece of their work. Partner outreach fails fastest when it sounds like a vendor pitch.

**Hook.** Show genuine engagement with their work. If you can't point to a specific report/post/talk, don't email this person cold.

**Credibility + why-now.** Position as peers, not as a vendor chasing a referral. The observation should genuinely connect to their view — don't stretch.

**CTA.** "Trade notes" is the right framing for partner outreach. Still propose a specific slot.

- `[Your role]`, `[your firm]`, `[your angle]` ← from `CONFIG.md`
- `[One specific observation from your firm's work]` ← must genuinely connect
- `[Client A], [Client B]` ← from `CONFIG.md`
- `[Specific day/time]`
- `[Your first name]`

---

## RULES

1. Never frame this as a sales email. If the CTA reads like one, rewrite.
2. The observation in paragraph 2 must actually relate to their stated view — not a generic line from your pitch.
3. If they respond, don't pivot to a pitch in the second email. That's how partner relationships die.
